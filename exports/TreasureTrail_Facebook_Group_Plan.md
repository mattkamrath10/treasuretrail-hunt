# TreasureTrail — Facebook Group Starter Kit & Posting Plan

A simple, step-by-step plan to launch a Facebook group that drives traffic to your
site and lines up with your 30-day SEO plan. Email this to yourself and follow it
in order.

---

## PART 1 — CREATE THE GROUP (Day 1, ~20 minutes)

1. On Facebook, go to **Menu (≡) → Groups → Create New Group**.
2. **Name it** (pick one):
   - *Central Valley Treasure Hunters — Estate Sales, Garage Sales & Flea Market Finds*
   - *Central Valley Estate Sales, Garage Sales & Treasure Hunting*
   - *Fresno–Bakersfield Treasure Trail: Sales, Finds & Reselling*
3. **Privacy:** choose **Public** (so posts can be found and shared widely).
4. **Add a cover photo:** use one of your new blog cover images, or your app logo.
5. Set yourself as **Admin**.

### Group description (copy/paste)
> Welcome to the Central Valley's home for treasure hunting! This is the community
> for estate sales, garage sales, flea markets, swap meets, auctions, collectibles,
> and reselling across Fresno, Bakersfield, Visalia, Hanford, Porterville, Madera,
> and all of Kern, Kings, Tulare, and Madera counties.
>
> Share your finds, post upcoming sales and events, ask for advice, and learn how to
> spot value before you buy. Powered by TreasureTrail — find and list local sales at
> treasuretrail-hunt.com.

### Group rules (copy/paste — add 4–5)
1. **Be kind and local.** Keep it friendly and Central Valley–focused.
2. **No spam or unrelated ads.** Posts must relate to sales, finds, or reselling.
3. **Real sales only.** No scams, fakes, or misleading listings.
4. **Give locations.** Include the city/county for sales and events.
5. **No harassment or hate.** Treat everyone with respect.

---

## PART 2 — SEED IT BEFORE INVITING PEOPLE (Day 1–2)

A new group looks empty. Post **3–5 things first** so it feels active:
1. A short **welcome post** (use the description above) pinned to the top.
2. One of your **blog articles** with a friendly intro (see post templates below).
3. A **"what's your best find?"** question to invite comments.
4. A link to the **app/site**: "Find and list local sales here → treasuretrail-hunt.com"

---

## PART 3 — INVITE & GROW (Day 2 onward)

1. Invite friends, family, and anyone local who likes thrifting/yard sales.
2. Share the group link in **other local Facebook groups** (where allowed) — e.g.
   Fresno/Bakersfield buy-sell-trade and yard-sale groups.
3. Ask your first members to invite one person each.
4. Post your group link on the **site and your other channels** so visitors can join.

---

## PART 4 — POSTING SCHEDULE (lines up with your 30-day plan)

Aim for **4–5 posts per week**. Rotate these types:

| Day | Post type | What to post |
|-----|-----------|--------------|
| Mon | **Blog article** | Share one of your 25 articles with a 1–2 line intro + the link |
| Wed | **Local sales/events** | Highlight upcoming sales or events from the app |
| Fri | **Tip or question** | A quick treasure-hunting tip, or "What did you find this week?" |
| Sat | **Find of the week** | Re-share a great member find, or post one yourself |
| Sun *(optional)* | **App nudge** | "List your sale free on TreasureTrail → treasuretrail-hunt.com" |

**Key rule for SEO:** every time you share a blog article, paste the **full article
link** (e.g. `treasuretrail-hunt.com/blog/estate-sales-fresno-county-guide`). Those
links send visitors to your site and help your articles get noticed by Google.

### Rotate your 25 articles
You have 25 articles — that's about **5–6 weeks** of Monday posts. Just go down the
list, then start over. Lead with the most useful ones first:
- Estate Sales in Fresno County
- Estate Sales in Bakersfield
- Garage sale guides (Visalia, Bakersfield, Hanford)
- Flea markets (Fresno, Tulare County)

## PART 6 — WHAT THIS DOES (and doesn't do) FOR GOOGLE

- ✅ **Keep submitting YOUR site's sitemap** to Google Search Console:
  `https://treasuretrail-hunt.com/sitemap.xml` (already includes all 25 articles).
- ✅ The Facebook group drives **real visitors** and **shared links** to your
  articles — that traffic and those links support your rankings over time.
- ❌ You **cannot** submit a Facebook group's sitemap to Google. Facebook controls
  that content; Google Search Console only accepts sitemaps for your own domain.

**Bottom line:** Google stays pointed at your website. The Facebook group is your
traffic engine that feeds people to it.
