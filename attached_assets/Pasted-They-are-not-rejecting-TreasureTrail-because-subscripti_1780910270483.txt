They are not rejecting TreasureTrail because subscriptions are forbidden, and they are not saying your subscription setup is technically broken. They are rejecting it under Guideline 3.1.2(c) – Auto-Renewable Subscriptions because Apple believes the required subscription disclosures are missing from either the app, the App Store metadata, or both.

Issue #1: Missing Terms of Use (EULA) Link
Summary

Apple states that apps offering auto-renewable subscriptions must provide users with a functional Terms of Use (EULA) link.

The reviewer specifically says the App Store metadata does not include the required Terms of Use information. Apple allows either the standard Apple EULA or your own custom EULA, but whichever option you choose must be properly linked in App Store Connect.

This is a documentation and compliance issue rather than a coding issue. Apple wants users to know the legal agreement governing the subscription before they subscribe.

What must be done
Option A (easiest)

Use Apple's standard EULA.

Add this link to the App Description:

https://www.apple.com/legal/internet-services/itunes/dev/stdeula/
Option B

Use TreasureTrail's own Terms of Use page.

Example:

https://treasuretrail-hunt.com/terms

Then enter that URL in the EULA section of App Store Connect.

Issue #2: Missing Subscription Information Inside the App
Summary

Apple reminds developers that auto-renewable subscriptions must clearly disclose subscription details inside the application itself.

The reviewer did not explicitly say which item is missing, but they listed the required information that must appear on the subscription screen.

This usually means Apple either couldn't find the information easily or one or more required pieces were absent.

Required Information Apple Lists

The subscription screen must clearly show:

Subscription Title

Subscription Length

Example:

Subscription Price

Example:

Privacy Policy Link

Example:

https://treasuretrail-hunt.com/privacy
Terms of Use Link

Example:

https://treasuretrail-hunt.com/terms
Issue #3: Missing Subscription Information in App Store Metadata
Summary

Apple requires subscription information not only inside the app but also inside App Store Connect.

Even if your app displays everything correctly, the App Store listing itself must contain the required legal links.

This is one of the most common subscription rejections.

What must be done

Verify App Store Connect contains:

Apple specifically asks that future submissions include information about where reviewers can find subscription information.

This is not the reason for rejection, but it is a recommendation to prevent future delays.
